Group S: Chinese Foreign Direct Investment

Chapter 2 Data Source

Since the Chinese government does not disclose detailed Chinese foreign investment data, studying Chinese foreign investment would reply on independent databases. In this study, we will mainly use two sources for Chinese foreign investment. One is the China Global Investment Tracker (CGIT) from the American Enterprise Institute (AEI). The other is the Global Chinese Official Finance Dataset from the AidData Lab at the College of William & Mary.

CGIT follows Chinese global activities the way the Chinese government does. China’s Ministry of Commerce (MOFCOM) publishes monthly data on Chinese outward investment and China’s total foreign contractual project value, two separate types of China’s development finance. In similar ways, CGIT tracks them separately and together as “investment” and “construction” projects. Being able to compare its total value to the regularly updated MOFCOM data, CGIT helps researchers understand how well it follows the official figure. So far, the Tracker has managed to retain a small gap from the official aggregate data published by the Chinese government.

The Geocoded Global Chinese Official Finance Dataset includes five datasets: one on China’s Official Development Assistance (ODA)-like projects, one on China’s Other Official Flows (OOF)-like projects, one on projects that are neither ODA-like nor OOF-like, one combining them all, and one with detailed project descriptions and sources for all projects. The reason projects are divided into ODA-like, OOF-like, or neither is that ODA and OOF are OECD countries definition. ODAs are traditionally referred as aids, whereas OOF are just investment. Since Chinese aids and investment do not use such a standard, researchers at AidData have to discern which are which by themselves so that the data could be comparable to western investment and aids.

Since the two databases cover different period. We will choose their overlaps, which is from 2015 to 2014. Since both databases collected information from media. We expect there would be difference between them. We use both of them because they provide different key information about Chinese foreign investment.
